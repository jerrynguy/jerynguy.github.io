Da Nang: Awarding scholarships to study abroad; 20 to 200 million VND for students

On January 20, Da Nang Study Encouragement Association announced that it together with "Center of English" awarded the scholarship "Nurturing dreams" for the 2020-2021 school year to students who overcame learning difficulties. Mr. Tran Dinh Lien - Chairman of the City Study Promotion Association shared, from 2019, the City Study Promotion Association started implementing the scholarship program "Nurturing dreams" for students from Da Nang in difficult circumstances. difficulties but efforts to overcome difficulties, fond of learning, is studying first year at public universities nationwide. In the year 2019-2020, 10 students with difficult backgrounds will receive scholarships totaling 200 million VND. Mr. Lien said that the scholarships are aimed at motivating the students to strive to study and practice to become good citizens and make a useful contribution to society. Students are eligible to receive scholarships until they graduate from university, with the condition of achieving good academic performance after each academic year. It is expected that the "Nurturing the dream" scholarship program will continue to be implemented in the following school years with the number of 10 students and the scholarship of 70 million VND for excellent students, 40 million VND. Da Nang, Quang Nam repaired schools, awarded scholarships to students in disaster areas

On January 18, the Da Nang Red Cross Association announced that it had coordinated with the Red Cross Association of Quang Nam province to organize activities to connect love, hand over and award the upgrade, renovate facilities and award scholarships to student in Quang Nam province, where damage is caused by natural disasters, floods. This is an activity in response to the program "School to School - Love Connection" and the Movement "Tet for the poor and Agent Orange victims" Xuan Tan Suu 2021 launched by the Central Vietnam Red Cross. The activity aims to connect, share and spread practical humanitarian values ​​to support schools, students and teachers in difficult circumstances in key areas of natural disasters. At Dai Son Primary School and Secondary School (TH-Secondary), Dai Loc District, Quang Nam Province, the program awarded scholarships and handed over works with a total value of more than 252 million VND. In particular, the program has repaired classrooms, libraries and toilets, repaired and built a football field, play area, game equipment, combined with sun and rain. Also at the program, the Quang Nam Red Cross Association and Nguyen Khuyen Secondary-High School, Da Nang, awarded 65 scholarships to disadvantaged pupils at Dai Son Secondary School, Dai Loc District, Quang Nam Province. This is a meaningful gift from organizations and sponsors to create better conditions for students and teachers of Dai Son school, Dai Loc district, Quang Nam province in teaching and learning, thereby motivating the spirit. , adding motivation for teachers and students of the school to overcome difficulties, to teach well, to study well.

Many Asian English Center facilities are not licensed: What does the director say?

On January 12, the Ho Chi Minh City Department of Education and Training announced a list of nearly 500 computer and foreign language centers in the city that have not been licensed and have expired educational activities. Notably in this list there are dozens of establishments of the Asian English Language Center - a famous foreign language center with 35 establishments in Ho Chi Minh City and many provinces across the country. Specifically, according to the list above, there are 9 Asian English Language Centers in District 12, Binh Tan District, Go Vap District, Hoc Mon District that have not been licensed to operate in education. 14 Asian English Center campuses in District 12, Tan Phu district, Nha Be district, Hoc Mon district, ... the license is expired. It is worth mentioning that although many institutions have not been licensed for educational activities, this Center still operates vigorously, advertising and recruiting students.
